Multi Pairwise Graphical

Pairwise graphical/numerical comparisons can be used to express your judgment about the relative importance or preference or likelihoods of the two elements shown on each line.

 

In the example below, we are asked to compare the relative importance of each pair of objectives with respect to the decision of which Leverage Knowledge.

 

 

For each pair, you can enter a judgment by dragging the slider bar or by entering a number (greater than 0) in the left or right boxes below the slider bar.

 

In the top line of the example above, a judgment is made that "Vendor/Partner Access" is about twice as important than "Customer/Access Service"  with respect to Leverage Knowledge.

 

You can drag the bar only up to ratio 9:1 as the extreme. Judgments with ratios > 9 to 1 can be entered numerically in the input boxes.

 

If you realize that your judgment is inverted, you can click on the icon.

 

After entering your judgment on the selected pair, the selection will automatically move to the next pair.

 

Your judgment will be automatically saved when you go to another step, such as by clicking the Next button.

 

Navigation:

The easiest and most common way to proceed through the evaluation is sequential -- by clicking the button.   You can go back to a previous step using the button. If you have previously entered judgments and requested to go back to an earlier step (see below) you click to jump to the next step that is un-assessed.

 

Non Sequential Navigation:

 

If the project manager enables displaying the navigation box, you can click on any step to move either forward or backward.

 

Depending on options set by the project manager, you may have considerable flexibility in navigating through the evaluation without sequentially going step by step.  We recommend that you do this only if you have used Comparion before.  

 

The icon  at the bottom left of the screen can be used to display a pop up a view of the hierarchy.  By clicking on any element in the hierarchy, you will jump to the first screen that elicits judgments' with respect to' that element. The 'with respect to' for the current step is shown in blue.

The number of steps (pages shown during the evaluation)  is NOT the same as the number of evaluations because:
